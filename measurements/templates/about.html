TOPSIS

The entire world has been adversely impacted due to the uncontrollable spread of Covid-19. This has given birth to unprecedented and unusual challenges not only for people and the government but for the healthcare sector as well. With the number of cases rising at an exponential rate, the need of quarantine centers and resources like sanitization services, rooms etc. sky rocketed. This paper focuses on solving the problem of Identifying Quarantine center location using Technique for Order Preference by Similarity (TOPSIS) which is a Multi-Criterion Decision-Making (MCDM) technique. Quarantine centers from Delhi and nearby areas and the factors related to them like distance of the quarantine center from the nearby covid hospitals, number of rooms available, services available, population in the region etc. were taken into consideration. TOPSIS has been used to calculate the closeness coefficient score of our alternatives (quarantine centers) and rank them based on the score obtained for each alternative from ‘the best optimal solution’ to the ‘least optimal solution’. Entropy method has been considered to calculate weights for each of the alternatives (quarantine center).


PSO

PSO is one of the most significant algorithms for solving problems like complex site detection, selection and allocation. Using different parameters we’ll be organizing the quarantine centers. Particle swarm optimization can be used for maximization and minimization as well. Moreover, few parameters such as distance which needs to be minimized and number of beds/rooms which are used to maximize.The goal of this study is to use PSO meta-heuristic algorithms for the location-allocation of the quarantine centers in Delhi NCR region. The final centers are selected and allocated using PSO meta-heuristic algorithms.
